Algorithm Recommendation

Basic Principles

We recommend content & topics based on users’ role, industry and activities with Gartner. Users’ activities include research content users read, topics that were followed, webinars views, discussions with Gartner. Recommendations depend on freshness of the content and the popularity of the content amongst similar users.

Our algorithm will refine overtime based on users’ continuous interactions with Gartner in order to provide high quality, decision-relevant content.

Computation Mechanism

We take users’ roles, industries, activities data (primarily users’ readership, topic follow data) to train deep learning recommendation models. Most models only update daily with some reacting close to real time to users’ actions.

Our models predict the most relevant content and topics that users are interested in after adjusting for user entitlements and use case specific suppressions.

Use Cases

Primarily used in:

  1. Recommended contents and topics on Gartner.com home page
  2. Recommended related contents within the context of research content
  3. Recommended contents delivered through in-app notifications & emails

Purposes

Surfacing high quality, decision-relevant contents to enable users make better and faster decisions and make progress on their priorities.
