If you’d like to make a request on behalf of another individual, please click here for more information.

PRIVACY OPTIONS
 
Request information about how Caesars uses your information
 
Residents of some countries and U.S. states can request information regarding (i) the categories of information we have collected about them, (ii) the categories of sources from which the information was collected, (iii) the business or commercial purpose for collecting, selling, or sharing the information, and (iv) the categories of third parties with whom we have shared personal information.
 
Request a copy of your information (right to access)
 
Residents of some countries and U.S. states can obtain a portable copy of the information that you have provided to us or that we have collected about you. We will provide a copy of information collected from January 1, 2022, through the date of your request, unless doing so would be impossible or would involve disproportionate effort. Eligible individuals may request a different time period, provided that the start date is no earlier than January 1, 2022.
 
Confirm if Caesars is using your information (right to confirm processing)
 
Residents of some U.S. states can confirm whether we are using/processing your information. “Processing” means any operation or set of operations performed on your information. Examples include collection, use, storage, disclosure, analysis, deletion, or modification of your information.
 
Delete your information
 
Residents of some countries and U.S. states can delete information we have in our records. There are limitations or restrictions on what we can delete, however. For example, we cannot delete information that we’re required by law to keep.
 
Limit use of your sensitive personal information
 
Residents of some U.S. states can limit our use of their sensitive personal information to the original purpose for which the information was collected and other authorized uses listed in state regulations. Sensitive personal information varies by state but can include a Social Security or driver’s license number, biometric data, precise geolocation data, racial or ethnic origin, and other information described in state privacy statutes.
 
Correct your information
 
Residents of certain countries and U.S. states have the legal right to correct inaccurate information in our records, subject to applicable legal exceptions. All Caesars Rewards members, regardless of residency, can also request corrections to account information through our privacy request form.
 
Prevent the sale of your information (‘do not sell’ request)
 
Residents of certain states can prevent the sale of their personal information. A “sale” under applicable state law may include sharing information with a third party in exchange for any valuable benefit—monetary or otherwise. We also offer this option to all Caesars Rewards members, regardless of residency. Please note that exercising this opt-out will not prevent a sale/transfer of your information to a third party in connection with a merger, acquisition, bankruptcy, or other sale of all or a portion of our assets. 
 
Prevent the sharing/use of your information for cross contextual behavioral advertising (targeted advertising)
 
Residents of certain states can prevent the use or sharing of their information for “cross contextual behavioral advertising” or “targeted advertising.” These are advertisements that are targeted to your interests, which are predicted based on your activities over non-Caesars websites, applications, or other services. Even if you exercise this opt-out, you will still see advertisements on our websites and mobile apps, including advertisements that are displayed to you based on your activities within our own websites, mobile apps, and/or other services we provide.
 
Prevent the automated processing of your information (profiling)
 
Residents of certain countries and U.S. states can prevent the use of automated processing of their information to evaluate, analyze, or predict their economic situation, health, personal preferences, interests, reliability, behavior, location, or movements if that processing is used by us to make a decision resulting in the provision or denial of financial and lending services, housing, insurance, education enrollment, criminal justice, employment opportunities, health care services, or access to basic necessities. We do not believe that we currently use automated processing for any of these purposes. However, we are providing an opt out right to all eligible persons in case these legal requirements or our practices change in the future.
 
Deactivate your account
 
Customers who do not have a legal right to delete their information can instead deactivate their Caesars Rewards account or online gaming account. While we will not delete your information if you request deactivation, we will adjust account settings so that your information will not be shared with our business partners, and you will no longer receive any marketing communications from us.
 
Appeals
 
Residents of certain U.S. states have the right to appeal our decision regarding a request related to the rights above. Confirm your eligibility below. If eligible, you may appeal our decision by emailing us at privacyquestions@caesars.com.
 
Non-Discrimination
 
Residents of certain U.S. states have the right not to receive discriminatory treatment for the exercise of your privacy rights. However, you may not be eligible for certain discounts and other benefits provided by our Caesars Rewards loyalty program if you exercise certain privacy rights. Please see our “Notice of Financial Incentive” below for more information.
