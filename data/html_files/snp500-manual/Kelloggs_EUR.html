Privacy Policy



At Kellogg, we believe in a just world and in nourishing the communities we serve. We are driven by our values of integrity, accountability, passion, humility, simplicity, and achieving results. That’s why we’re also committed to treating your personal information responsibly and making sure that you always know how your information is used. 


The purpose of this policy is to inform you how Kellogg Europe Trading Limited (“Kellogg”) uses your personal data. 

1.    General


Kellogg uses personal data (information which can identify you) in a number of ways. Whenever we use this data, we try to be as transparent as possible to let you know what we’re doing and why. This data can be gathered and used in a variety of ways, such as when you access our website and your device communicates with our server and provides some information about you with your consent. 


We implement security measures to ensure your personal data is kept safe and secure at all times and ensure that no matter where your data is processed, that your rights are respected.


Kellogg, as part of a worldwide group of companies (“Kellogg Group”), may transfer your personal data within the Kellogg Group as part of its processing operations. We may also use external data processors in various different countries. If your personal data is transferred and processed elsewhere within the Kellogg Group, or is processed by an external processor, we will make sure that your rights are preserved and that any processing is carried out lawfully and only under our instructions for the stated purpose/s under the terms of a data processing agreement. If your data is transferred out of the EEA, we ensure that such transfers are grounded on EU approved Standard Contractual Clauses. 


We are committed to keeping your data safe and secure and have implemented such security tools in our online platforms such as Captchas and Multi-Factor Authentication. In some cases, personal data may be required to implement these measures, however whenever this is required, we will ask your permission and will not retain data obtained for this purpose.


This privacy policy will be updated from time to time by Kellogg. While we will usually not specifically draw your attention to changes, if we feel that the changes are material to your rights, we will take steps to ensure you are informed of these (i.e. by notification on our website or by email). 

2.    Our Website and Apps


When visiting and using our website or apps, if you do not register or otherwise provide us with your consent for use of cookies (small data files which are placed on your device and can be used to identify you), we will only use data that your browser transmits to our server which is technically necessary for us to display our website to you and to guarantee stability and security (i.e. strictly necessary cookies). 


We may process the following types of data in connection with our website or apps:


·         Your IP address

·         The website you visited prior to landing on our web page

·         Time and date of website access

·         The pages you visit, time spent, and actions taken on our site

·         HTTP code

·         Information on the browser and operating system you’re using


This type of personal data is processed on the basis of our legitimate interests for the above purposes and is typically stored for about 7 days. Data we collect may be transferred or processed within the Kellogg Group or by external service processors (i.e. for hosting or content management) in accordance with the above purposes. 


If you have enabled geolocation settings on your browser, various services on our websites or other applications may request this information in order to provide you with a service that you request. Any location information will only be used for the purposes that you have requested with your consent.

3.    Cookies


When you use any of Kellogg’s online services, such as our website or apps, we use cookies—small data files which are placed on your device—for a variety of reasons. Some cookies are technically necessary in order to ensure our website or apps display or function correctly. Any cookies which are classified as technically necessary are processed in our legitimate interest and are usually active for in or around 7 days. These cookies cannot be deactivated individually, but you can still set your browser settings to reject all cookies should you desire.


Cookies may contain information about your device and preferences which is then communicated to our server. While with certain third-party cookies, these may be used to collect information about your browsing history across multiple websites (for which we will always seek your consent beforehand), for cookies which are required for functionality or performance, these will only collect pseudonymized data and will not collect information concerning your browsing preferences.


You can view each type of cookie we use on our website, together with its purpose and how long it’s implemented for in our Cookie Preferences  tool.  


Strictly Necessary Cookies


These cookies are required in order to display our website correctly and to enable it to function and they cannot be rejected. You can set your browser to reject all cookies using the instructions below, however this will prevent our website from functioning correctly. We process these cookies in our legitimate interest of providing functional online services and they will generally not involve the processing of your personal information. 


Functional & Performance Cookies


Upon implementation of functional and performance cookies, we receive the following categories of information concerning your use:


·         Number of visits to each page on our website

·         Number of subpages accessed

·         Time spent on the website

·         The order of pages visited

·         Your country and city (based on your IP address)

·         Movement, clicks and scrolling


We will only implement these cookies with your consent. They are used to enable our website to provide enhanced functionality allows us to measure the use of our website so that we can improve features and performance. The IP address of your computer is automatically made anonymous and will not allow us to draw any conclusions about any individual user. In addition, if you use any of the below browsers, you can follow the below links to find out how to deactivate cookies altogether:


Google Chrome 

Microsoft Edge 

Mozilla Firefox 

Apple Safari 


If you use a different type of browser, please consult your browser’s help and support function in order to locate the settings to disable cookies.


NOTE: Blocking cookies may prevent some pages from displaying or functioning correctly.


Targeting and Social Media Cookies


Certain cookies operate to gather information about your usage habits and interests by collecting information about the websites you visit and creating user profiles in order to target advertisements to you based on your interests. These types of cookies are primarily operated by external advertising companies (i.e. Google & Meta), but may be implemented when you visit our site. These cookies will not be implemented on our website unless you specifically consent to their use. You can withdraw your consent at any time by accessing Cookie Preferences .


Kellogg engages with operators of targeting cookies in its advertising activities and is responsible for any data that it processes independently of the cookie operators. The categories of data that are processed when advertising cookies are implemented are as follows:


·         IP address

·         Individual user IDs assigned by third party advertising companies (i.e. Google & Meta)

·         Potential product interests and usage profiles

·         Browsing patterns


IP addresses are anonymized in most cases and can generally not be used to locate individuals specifically.


Kellogg uses the following online advertising tools:


Google Ads – Uses cookies to draw attention to our products and offers across external websites. Kellogg receives aggregated data and can organize specific ad campaigns which is implemented by Google through ad servers. When you consent to use of advertising cookies on our website, an ad server cookie permits Google to be able to serve specific ads, including those of our products to you. These cookies are typically operational for about 30 days. 


Google Ads Remarketing – This is a function of the Google Ads service which allows us to present advertisements to you based on your interests across other websites within Google’s ad network. Cookies are used which monitor user interactions across multiple websites and then builds profiles of user behavior to show advertisements which are personally targeted. These cookies may last up to 180 days. 


Meta Pixel – Uses cookies for remarketing and retargeting of advertisements to users across the Meta network (i.e. other websites that have implemented ad serving through Meta), including Facebook. This is used to build user profiles based on their website interactions and target them with personalized advertisements tailored to their perceived preferences. Also contributes to analytics for ad effectiveness. 


Google Tag Manager – Implements website tags and may gather certain information such as access time and IP address. No cookies are implemented. 


Other Targeting Cookies - Google Analytics


Our website uses Google Analytics. Google Analytics is a web analysis service from Google which provides websites with usage monitoring technology and advertising supports. This is done by using cookies to analyze user activity on our website. This is done by the implementation of a unique cookie on user devices which sends information on your usage to Google’s servers in the USA. Google has implemented various technologies to ensure that your data is kept private and the data stored by it cannot be traced to you personally. For example, IP addresses are masked within the EEA to ensure that except in limited circumstances a full IP address is not stored by Google or transmitted to the USA.   


Google uses this information on our behalf to analyze your use of our website in order to compile reports on website activities and provide additional services related to website and internet use. The IP address transmitted by your browser in the context of Google Analytics is not merged with other Google data. Google Analytics is also used to build user profiles based on browsing habits so that advertisements can be targeted or re-targeted to specific users across various sites that have implemented Google advertising technologies. 


We use Google Analytics to analyze and regularly improve the usage of our website. We also make use of targeting and re-targeting advertisements to users. Statistics obtained are used by us to improve our online offering and personalize content to our users. In addition, we gain information about the functionality of our site (for example to detect navigation problems). Kellogg may also share any data obtained within the Kellogg Group 


Any data gathered through Google Analytics and associated marketing tools is held by Google in the USA. Please see Google’s privacy policy: https://policies.google.com/privacy?hl=en.


Cookies in relation to Google Analytics will only be implemented with your consent for the above purposes. 


You can opt-out of Google Analytics directly with Google. Information on how to do this can be found here . You can also set your browser to entirely reject cookies using information provided above. 


Other Targeting Cookies – Adobe Analytics


We have also implemented Adobe Analytics cookies on our website which provides us with analysis and insights into your use of our website similar to other analytics cookies. This technology analyses your interactions across ours and other websites with Adobe Analytics in order to understand your interests and provide more relevant marketing messages to you. As part of this analysis, data in relation to your interactions, device, and  IP address is used. 


For more information on Adobe analytics, you please see Adobe’s privacy policy: https://experienceleague.adobe.com/docs/analytics/technotes/privacy-overview.html?lang=en


You can opt out of Adobe analytics directly with adobe. Information on how to do this can be found here . You can also set your browser to reject cookies by default using the information provided above.

Social media platforms may use the above information to build usage profiles and customize their offering and/or advertising to you. 12.  Your Rights


Where we are processing your data based on your consent, you have the right to revoke your consent at any time. Any data we continue to hold on this basis will then be deleted. 


You also have the following rights:


o   The right to access your personal data held by us

o   The right to the erasure of your data in certain circumstances

o   The right to have any data we hold rectified if it is incorrect

o   The right to apply for a restriction of the processing of your data in certain circumstances

o   The right to object to the processing of your data in certain circumstances

o   The right to data portability of your data


You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the data protection supervisory authority. Please note that the lead supervisory authority with respect to Kellogg is the Data Protection Commission in Ireland. 


Access


Where you request access to your personal data held by Kellogg, we may seek some further information from you in relation to your request, including a requirement to provide proof of identity and entitlement to request access. We will endeavor to process any such requests as soon as possible and in any event within one month. Should it be required to extend this time, we will let you know in advance. 


Erasure


Where you request that we delete your data, we may seek further information from you in relation to your request, including proof of your identity and entitlement to request such erasure. We will comply with such requests in the following circumstances:


·         Where the personal data is no longer necessary for the purpose it was collected

·         Where you revoke your consent to data processed solely on this basis

·         Where you have objected to processing in circumstances where we rely on our legitimate interest and no compelling legitimate grounds exist to continue processing activities

·         Your data has been unlawfully processed

·         Erasure is required to fulfil a legal obligation


Rectification


Where data held by us contains errors or is otherwise incorrect, you have the right to request that it be correct. In certain circumstances you may also have the right to supplement it with further information that you wish to provide. 


Restriction of Processing


You have the right to apply to us for a restriction of processing in the following circumstances:


·         Where you dispute the correctness of the data held by us

·         Where the processing is unlawful 

·         The data is no longer required by the controller but is required by you for the exercise or defense of a legal claim

·         You have raised an objection to processing where it is not certain whether the legitimate grounds relied upon by the controller override your objection


Right to Object to Processing


You may object to the processing of your data in circumstances where it is being processed by us under our legitimate interest where you have particular grounds for such objection. Any such objection shall be assessed based on the existence any potential compelling legitimate grounds to continue the processing in the circumstances.
