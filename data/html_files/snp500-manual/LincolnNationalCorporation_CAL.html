California Privacy Notice

Print the California Privacy Notice

We are providing this California Privacy Notice pursuant to the California Consumer Privacy Act (“CCPA”) and the California Privacy Rights Act (“CPRA”). The California Privacy Notice describes how Lincoln Financial Group companies* (“Lincoln,” “us,” “we,” or “our”) may collect, use, share, and retain Personal Information for California residents, including information we collect online from California residents who visit our Lincoln websites (the “Sites”).

This California Privacy Notice applies to California residents who: (1) are customers or potential customers whose Personal Information is not already covered by our financial or health information privacy notices, individuals who visit our Sites, or individuals who request information about our products and services (“Consumers”); or (2) submit applications for employment with us (“Applicants”); or (3) enter into an employee (including former employees and retirees), independent contractor, or temporary worker relationship with us, or belong to our network of financial professionals, (comprised of insurance agents, registered representatives, and investment advisor representatives (hereinafter “Employees” solely for the purpose of describing their privacy rights under the CCPA and CPRA).

If you are a Consumer, Applicant, or Employee (“you” or “your”), “Personal Information” means information that identifies, relates to, describes, is reasonably capable of being associated with, or could reasonably be linked, directly or indirectly with you or your household. Personal Information does not include information that is publicly available, de-identified, or aggregated.

Lincoln respects the privacy and security of all Personal Information entrusted to us. This Notice supplements our other privacy policies, statements, and notices, including our Online and Mobile Privacy Notice, which can be found at: Online and Mobile Privacy Notice, and our Privacy Practices Notice for Lincoln Financial Group Employees, Job Applicants, and Financial Professionals, which can be found at: Privacy Practices Notice for Lincoln Financial Group Employees, Job Applicants, and Financial Professionals. In the event of a conflict between this Notice, and any other policy, statement, or notice, this Notice will prevail for residents of California.

We do not have actual knowledge that we sell or share the Personal Information of Consumers under the age of 16. We do not use or disclose Sensitive Personal Information for purposes to which the right to limit use and disclosure applies under the CCPA and CPRA.

We collect Consumer Personal Information from the following categories of sources:

  • You, if you are a customer or potential customer, submit an inquiry to us, complete an online form, or participate in a survey.
  • Automatically, through cookies, web beacons, and other technologies used on our Sites.
  • Our commercial customers or plan sponsors, if you participate in one of our retirement plans or receive certain group benefits through your employer.
  • Other sources, such as companies that help us maintain the accuracy and completeness of our data, help enhance our records.

We may disclose Consumer Personal Information to the following categories of recipients:

  • Service providers. We may disclose Consumer Personal Information to vendors, service providers, contractors or agents who perform functions on our behalf. These service providers may include financial service providers (e.g., third-party administrators, claims services, broker-dealers, insurance agents and brokers, and credit bureaus), as well as service providers that assist us in maintaining our network, and with customer service and marketing.
  • Joint marketers. We may disclose Consumer Personal Information to financial services companies with which we have a joint marketing agreement for the purpose of marketing our products and services to you as well as products and services that we jointly offer to you.
  • Business transfers. If we are acquired by or merged with another company, if substantially all of our assets are transferred to another company, or as part of a bankruptcy proceeding, we may transfer Consumer Personal Information to the other company.
  • Third parties. We may sell or share Consumer Personal Information to third parties for cross-context behavioral advertising and other marketing and advertising services that we utilize on our Sites.
  • Other recipients. We may disclose Consumer Personal Information where necessary to comply with the law, or in response to a judicial proceeding, court order, or other legal process, such as pursuant to a subpoena.

The retention periods for each category of Consumer Personal Information vary depending on the type of business record in which it is maintained. The retention period for our business records is set based on the length of time Lincoln needs the record for: (1) its original purpose; (2) other operational purposes, such as audits and reporting; and/or (3) legal compliance, including maintenance of opt-out/in lists, legal defense, and legal holds.

Your California privacy rights

If you are a resident of California, you have the right to submit certain requests relating to your Personal Information, as more fully described below. To exercise any of these rights, please complete and submit a request through our secure webform here: California Consumer Rights Request Form. When you submit a webform privacy rights request, we will require that you verify your identity before we can process your request.

  • If you have an existing relationship with us, or had a relationship with us in the past, a Lincoln representative will reach out to you by phone and request that you provide two or three pieces of personal information to verify your identity.
  • If you do not have a relationship with Lincoln, we will ask you to verify your identity through a secure, online automated identity verification tool.

California residents can also call us at 877-ASK-LINC (877-275-5462) to submit a privacy rights request. During that call, we will verify your identity prior to processing your request.

We will confirm receipt of your request and respond to your request as soon as we can and no later than legally required. You may designate an authorized agent to make a request on your behalf; however, we may require proof that you gave the agent permission to submit the request, and you will still need to verify your identity directly with us before we can process your request. Regardless of the method used to submit a request to access, delete, or correct your personal information, we must deny your request if we are unable to verify your identity.

If you believe that Personal Information, we maintain about you is inaccurate, you have the right to request that we correct that information.

Consumers have the right to opt out of the sale or sharing of their Personal Information, which we may do solely for our advertising technology programs such as through cookies for cross-context behavioral advertising, or other marketing and advertising services that we utilize on our Sites. To opt out, please select the “Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information” link in the footer of our Sites. Additionally, you may set your web browser’s “do not track,” or other similar browser-level or device-level mechanism, to signal to our Sites a request to disable certain tracking technologies (e.g., cookies) on our Sites. Refer to the browser or device mechanism for more information on how the mechanism interacts with which types of tracking technologies. Note that disabling certain types of tracking technologies may result in some features on our Sites to not function. Also note that we do not control these mechanisms and therefore our Sites will adhere to such signals to the extent reasonably technologically possible.

If you choose to exercise any of the privacy rights described above, we will not discriminate against you. This means that, consistent with applicable state privacy law, we will not deny providing our products or services to you, charge you different prices or provide a different level or quality of products or services to you unless those differences are related to the value of your Personal Information. You also have the right not to receive discriminatory treatment by us, including retaliation against you.
