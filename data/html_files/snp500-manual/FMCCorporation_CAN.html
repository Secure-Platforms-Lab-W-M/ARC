Summary

This Policy describes what types of information, namely personal Information, we gather and track about website visitors, visitors who submit information to us through our social media pages, individuals who provide us information at trade shows or other events, current and prospective vendors and suppliers, and the direct and indirect customers who purchase our products. We use this information principally to conduct our business and provide information about our products. We share this information within FMC, and with third parties that supply services to us or with whom we do business. If you have any questions about this Policy, contact DataPrivacy@fmc.com.

Information We May Collect About You

We may collect one or more of the following categories of information from and about you:

  • Personal Information that we can reasonably use to directly or indirectly identify you, such as your name, mailing address, e-mail address, telephone number, job title & role, Internet protocol (IP) address used to connect your device to the Internet, user name or other similar identifier, billing and account information, signature, language preference and any other identifier by which you may be contacted online or offline. We may also collect other Personal Information from or about you in connection with particular programs, events, or to provide you with requested goods or services. For instance, if you participate in one of our marketing programs, such as a grower rebate program, we may collect personal information from or about you to ensure we can provide you with the benefits of such program, such as your purchase history and rebate eligibility.
  • Non-Personal Information that does not directly or indirectly reveal your identity or directly relate to an identifiable individual, such as statistical or aggregated information. Statistical or aggregated data does not directly identify a specific person, but we may derive non-personal statistical or aggregated data from Personal Information. For example, we may aggregate Personal Information to calculate the percentage of users accessing a specific Website feature.
  • Technical Information including information about your internet connection, the equipment you use to access our Website, about your browser (including type and version), time zone setting, browser plug-in types and versions, operating system and platform, and usage details.
  • Non-personal details about your Website interactions including URLs and your clickstream to, through and from our Website (including date and time), how long you remain on a page and which links you click, products you viewed or searched for, page response times, download errors, methods used to browse away from the page, or any phone number used to call our customer service number;

We collect information about you in the following ways:

Information You Provide to Us

We collect information you knowingly provide by soliciting goods or services from us, as part of a transaction, performing or entering into a contract with us, completing an online form, submitting a query or request for support to us, registering for information or services from us, registering for an event we are involved with, responding to research and marketing surveys, searching or otherwise providing on or through our Website, or otherwise share with us.

The information we collect directly from you on, through or in connection with our Website may include:

  • Information you provide by filling in forms on our Website. This includes information provided at the time of subscribing to receive communications from us or using our Contact Us form.
  • Records and copies of your correspondence (including email and postal addresses), if you contact us.
  • Your responses to surveys that we might ask you to complete for research purposes.
  • Your search queries on the Website.

Information We Receive from Other Sources (e.g., Third Parties)

We may also collect Personal Information indirectly from our business partners. This may include information we receive from distributors or retailers of FMC products. We may need to obtain your information from our business partners to help address a question you’ve asked via our Website, or to provide after-sale services. Also, we may combine information we have collected directly from you with information we obtain from business partners or other companies and use it for the reasons described in this Policy.

In those cases, we generally ask that our business partners adhere to the privacy practices described in this Policy. Nevertheless, we’re not responsible for the privacy practices of our business partners and strongly encourage you to visit the privacy policy of our business partners’ website prior to providing your Personal Information.

FMC also collects information from visitors to the Website, from readers who have downloaded one of FMC’s mobile apps or visited our social network pages, and from recipients of our e-mail communications.

This includes a passive collection of information that is automatically sent to us by your web browser in order to customize your experience on our Website. For these purposes we may collect (depending on whether you are using the Website, a social network or an app) certain information about your equipment, browsing actions, and patterns, including:

  • Details of your visits to our Website, including traffic data, location data, logs, and other communication data, and the resources that you access and use on the Website
  • Information about your computer and internet connection, including your IP address, operating system, and browser type.

The information we collect automatically is statistical information and may include Personal Information, and we may maintain it or associate it with Personal Information we collect in other ways, that you provide to us, or that we receive from third parties. It helps us to improve our Website and deliver a better and more personalized service, including by enabling us to:

  • Estimate our audience size and usage patterns;
  • Develop content based on user interests and behavior;
  • Store information about your preferences, allowing us to customize our Website according to your individual interests;
  • Measure traffic patterns, including which areas of our Website users visit, and visiting patterns in the aggregate;
  • Understand how our users’ habits are similar or different from one another; and
  • Recognize when you return to our Website.

We collect this information by using cookies, server logs, and other similar technologies. For more information, see Cookies, Pixels and Automatic Data Collection Technologies.

Consent

To the extent required by law, we will obtain your consent to collect, use or disclose Personal Information. For example, we may collect Personal Information without your knowledge or consent if we have a serious and legitimate reason and either of the following conditions is fulfilled:

  • the information is collected in your interest and cannot be otherwise collected from you in due time; or
  • collection from a third person is necessary to ensure the accuracy of the information.

When required, consent may be provided orally, in writing, electronically or otherwise. Consent to the collection, use or disclosure of your Personal Information must be free and enlightened. Depending on the nature and sensitivity of your Personal Information, your consent can be implied or express. Your consent may also be given by an authorized representative (such as a person having power of attorney). By providing Personal Information to us, you agree that we may collect, use and disclose such Personal Information as set out in this Policy and as otherwise permitted or required by law.

In cases where we rely on your consent to lawfully collect, use and/or disclose your Personal Information, you may have the legal right to withdraw your consent at any time, provided that reasonable notice of withdrawal of consent is given to FMC. Please note that if you withdraw your consent, we may not be able to provide you with particular products or services for which that information is necessary.

Limits on Collection of Personal Information

We only collect Personal Information as authorized or required by law, where it is reasonable and necessary to provide our services, and where it is reasonable and necessary for the purposes consented by you.

Cookies, Pixels and Automatic Data Collection Technologies

As you navigate through and interact with our Website, we may use cookies, pixels and other automatic data collection technologies to collect certain information about your equipment, browsing actions, and patterns, as described in this Policy and our Cookie Policy.  Currently, our systems do not recognize browser “do-not-track” requests. You may, however, disable certain tracking as discussed in this section (e.g., by disabling cookies).

The technologies we use for this automatic data collection may include:

  • Cookies (or browser cookies).  A cookie is a small amount of data that is sent to your browser from a web server and stored on your computer’ shard drive or temporary memory. We may use this information to better personalize the content, banners, and promotions that you and other users will see on our Website. We may also work with third-party ad companies and ad networks.

You may change your cookie settings on your internet browser or delete our cookies at any time. You may also refuse to accept browser cookies by activating the appropriate setting on your browser. However, please note that if you disable cookies, all or part(s) of our Website may become inaccessible and certain features may not work correctly. Unless you adjust your browser settings to refuse cookies or decline cookies on our cookie banner, our system will issue them. For detailed information on the cookies we use, the purposes for which we use them, and how you can exercise your choices regarding our use of your cookies, see our Cookie Policy.

  • Web Beacons. Our Website pages and e-mails may contain web beacons (small transparent embedded images or objects, also known as clear gifs, pixel tags, and single-pixel gifs) that permit us, for example, to count website page visitors or email readers, or to compile other related statistics such as recording Website content popularity or verifying system and server integrity.
