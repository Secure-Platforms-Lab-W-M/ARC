Our Role as a Processor or Service Provider

Data protection laws sometimes differentiate between “controllers” and “processors” of personal data. A “controller” determines the purposes and means (or the why and the how) of processing personal data. A “processor,” which is sometimes referred to as a “service provider,” processes personal data on behalf of a controller subject to the controller’s instructions.

When our customers use the products and services they purchase from us, we may  process personal data on behalf of our customers (the controllers)  in accordance with their instructions and, therefore, act as a “processor.” If you have questions about personal data that may be processed by Motorola Solutions as a “processor” in connection with the products or services used by one of our customers, or want to exercise any of your rights regarding such personal data, we recommend that you contact the applicable customer directly as it will be our obligation to redirect any inquiry you make to us back to that Motorola Solutions customer.

Personal Data Collected

What personal data is collected?

Product and Services

When you place an order with us, we may collect personal data about you, including name, title, company, address, email address, phone numbers and, in rare instances, other categories of personal data, such as payment card information, driver’s license numbers, social security numbers (or equivalent national identification numbers) and date of birth.

Marketing

We may ask you for and collect personal data at other times in connection with our marketing activities, including when you enter a sweepstakes or contest; participate in a promotion or marketing campaign sponsored by us or one of our business partners; when you want to find out about services offered in your area; when you complete surveys or other questionnaires; when you participate in a user forum or blog hosted by us or one of our business partners; when you send us an email.

We (and our Service Providers) may also collect information made publicly available through third-party platforms such as social media websites, online databases, or online directories.

If you do not wish to receive marketing communications you can opt out by visiting the Communications Preference Center.
